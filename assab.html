Rania Assab

post doc

rania.assab@iplesp.upmc.fr

Bio: Rania studied biomedical sciences at Paris Descartes University and bioinformatics at Université de Bordeaux. She did her PhD at Conservatoire National des arts et métiers and Institut Pasteur where she modelled multi-scale nosocomial pathogens transmission (norovirus in nursing homes and vancomycin-resistant enterococci in hospital) using real contact and transfer network data. She then implemented strategies to assess cost-effective combinations. Since then, she held a postdoc position in genomics where she annotated and identified genomic signatures of Mycobacteria abscessus in order to better understand evolution and pathogenesis (antibiotic resistance and virulence factors).

Research activities: Rania is specialized in mathematical modelling of pathogens transmission, antimicrobial resistance epidemiology and genomics. She currently works on covid19 transmission modelling in order to explore solutions, strategies against its spread.
