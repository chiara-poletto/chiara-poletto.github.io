For master students

I welcome motivated Master’s students interested in infectious disease dynamics, mathematical and physics modeling, and data-driven approaches to public health. I supervise Master’s thesis projects that combine quantitative methods with real-world data, drawing on tools from mathematics, physics, statistics, and computational modeling. Projects can be adapted to the student’s background and interests and may involve data analysis, modeling, or simulation. Students interested in pursuing a research-oriented Master’s thesis are encouraged to get in touch to discuss possible topics and supervision.

I am also happy to advise students interested in computational epidemiology and computational social science to choose their internship outside the University in Padova, connecting them with labs in Italy or abroad. Labs I am connected with include: EPIcx, ISI Foundation, FBK, Dondena Center, and many others
